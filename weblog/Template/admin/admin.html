Quote

Freedom is the will to be responsible to ourselves. It is to preserve the distance which separates us from other men. To grow more indifferent to hardship, to severity, to privation, and even to life itself. To be proud and sure of ourselves without selfishness. To be so free in spirit that we can truly love the one we love. To apprehend the beauty of the earth in silence. To feel the joy of solitude. To be able to withstand criticism without resentment and to refuse praise without arrogance. To be able to hear, to see, and to feel, and never to forget that these sensations are a gift from God and constitute the real meaning of life.

Michael Oakeshott
